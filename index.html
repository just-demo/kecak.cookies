History of Kecak Dance

Kecak Dance is one of the unique dance of Bali, this dance is not accompanied by musical instruments or gambelan, but only accompanied by a chorus of about 40 - 70 men or more. Kecak Dance originated from the sacred dance (Sang Hyang). In Sang Hyang Dance someone who had the trance can communicate with the gods or ancestors, the dancer as a media to do that and then the dancer can convey the god or ancestors's. Since the 1930's the epic story of ramayana is inserted at the Kecak Dance.

Once upon time, because common evil of Dewi Kakayi (Stepmother of Sri Rama), the Sri Rama prince of the kingdom Ayodya is exiled from his father's palace Majesty Dasarata. Sri Rama accompanied by his younger brother "Laksamana" and his faithful wife "Sita", went to Dandaka forestry.

When at the forest, The Sri Rama's place is known by King Dasamuka "Rahwana" in which a despotic king. Rahwana was captivated by the beauty of Dewi Sita, and he tried to kidnap hers, assisted by his governor, Marica. With his power giant Marica was transformed into a beautiful golden deer and agile. And with that way they managed to separate Sita from Rama and Laksamana. Rahwana then use this opportunity to kidnap Sita and take hers to Alengka Pura. Rama and Laksamana were helped by the monkey's army that under commander of monkey Hanoman. They success defeat the Rahwana's giant reinforcements led by Meganada (Rahwana's son). Rama finally won her back safely.
